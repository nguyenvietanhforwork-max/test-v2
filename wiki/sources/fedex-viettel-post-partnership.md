# FedEx Bets on Viettel Post to Strengthen Capabilities in Vietnam

> VIR interview with FedEx N&S Pacific president Masamichi Ujiie on the
> April-2026 partnership making Viettel Post the National Network Provider
> for FedEx in Vietnam — combining FedEx's global network with Viettel
> Post's nationwide infrastructure to win cross-border e-commerce growth.

## Summary

This Vietnam Investment Review Q&A (28 Apr 2026) with Masamichi Ujiie,
president of FedEx North & South Pacific, explains a partnership effective
26 April 2026 in which Viettel Post becomes the **National Network
Provider** for FedEx in Vietnam. Under the model, Viettel Post handles
domestic pickup, delivery, warehousing, and customs coordination; FedEx
owns international transportation, end-to-end service standards, and
advanced logistics solutions [§Division of responsibilities].

Ujiie frames the deal as filling Vietnam-specific gaps in FedEx's operating
model: scalability, flexibility, and seamless first-mile-to-last-mile
connectivity in a fast-evolving market. Viettel Post is positioned as the
right partner thanks to nationwide coverage, operational scale, and a
shared technology orientation [§Strategic rationale]. The bet is on
Vietnam's fast-growing cross-border e-commerce sector: broader coverage,
extended pickup cut-offs, and shorter transit times mean SMEs can scale
international operations with more confidence [§Cross-border e-commerce].

Integration challenges, Ujiie acknowledges, are process and system
alignment: ensuring real-time information flow across both networks, and
consistent service performance across geographies and operating
environments. Technology investments — FedEx data from ~17 million daily
shipments fed into AI-enabled visibility tools like FedEx Surround — are
positioned as enablers, not replacers, of human work; the workforce shift
is toward analysis, system management, and customer solutions [§Smart
technologies].

The interview closes with a tactical note: contingency plans for the
upcoming long-holiday demand surge, leveraging real-time visibility to
maintain service quality.

## Deep Dive

**The deal structure.** Effective 26 April 2026, Viettel Post serves as
FedEx's National Network Provider for Vietnam. The agreement carves
responsibilities clearly: Viettel Post is responsible for domestic pickup
and delivery, warehouse operations, and customs clearance coordination.
FedEx retains international transportation, end-to-end service standards
across the global network, and the design of logistics and digital
solutions targeted at the Vietnam market. The intent is to integrate
FedEx's global network with Viettel Post's domestic infrastructure into a
seamless end-to-end supply chain [§Division of responsibilities; closing
boilerplate].

**Strategic rationale (FedEx side).** Ujiie's argument is that customer
expectations have outgrown speed alone; the market wants reliability,
resilience, and end-to-end visibility across borders. FedEx has been in
the Vietnamese market for four decades and now needs a partner that brings
nationwide scale, operational depth, and aligned technology values to
match its global standards. Viettel Post fits that bill — nationwide
coverage, an established fleet, and a domestic player with which FedEx can
align processes and adopt international service standards [§Strategic
rationale].

**Strategic rationale (market-shaping).** Ujiie frames the deal as a
benchmark-setter for Vietnam's logistics industry: by raising expectations
on reliability, transparency, speed and connectivity, the partnership
"sets the standard for both international and domestic players" to
continue innovating. The implicit positioning is that Vietnam's logistics
market is consolidating around globally-connected, integrated providers
[§Competition reshaping].

**Operational integration challenges.** The article surfaces two concrete
challenges. First, complexity management: ensuring near real-time
information flow across the two systems so customers see consistent
visibility end-to-end. Second, performance consistency across different
operating environments and geographies at scale. The mitigation toolkit:
aligning operational processes, adopting international standards, and
strengthening coordination across pickup, customs clearance, and delivery
[§Integration challenges].

**Cross-border e-commerce thesis.** Vietnam's cross-border e-commerce
sector is the explicit growth catalyst. The partnership promises broader
coverage, earlier deliveries, and extended pickup cut-off times. Even
small improvements in delivery timing, Ujiie notes, translate into
inventory and responsiveness gains — particularly for SMEs that lack scale
to absorb friction. Enhanced visibility and tracking are framed as the
SME-enabling layer [§Cross-border e-commerce].

**Smart-technology framing.** Two AI-related claims stand out. First,
FedEx processes ~17 million shipments daily; scan and shipment data feed
analytics that customers consume via tools such as FedEx Surround for
disruption forecasting. Second, AI and digital tools are positioned as
"enablers that strengthen rather than replace operations." Workforce
implications: routine work automates, and human roles shift toward data
analysis, system management, and customer solutions [§Smart technologies].

**Quality assurance.** Service quality is to be measured and ensured
through tight process alignment and FedEx's global standards on shipment
management, tracking, and service performance. The piece notes ongoing
contingency planning for an upcoming long-holiday demand surge, framed
through the lens of real-time visibility and continuous performance
monitoring [§Service quality].
