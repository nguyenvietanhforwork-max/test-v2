# VASEP 2026 USD 12bn Seafood Export Target

## Executive Summary
The Vietnam Association of Seafood Exporters and Processors (VASEP) is publicly targeting USD 12bn in seafood exports for 2026 and lobbying for removal of non-tariff trade barriers, faster customs/inspection cycles, and tariff relief in key markets. The target implies double-digit YoY growth and depends on a recovery in pangasius demand in China alongside firm US shrimp pricing.

## Key Insights
- The 12bn target represents ~12% YoY growth from estimated 2025 levels, requiring price *and* volume to cooperate.
- VASEP's policy ask focuses on three vectors: streamlined export inspections, anti-dumping duty mitigation in the US, and faster certification of additional China-approved facilities.
- A single-cycle headwind (e.g., second-half shrimp price softness or a renewed pangasius duty review) could clip 5–8% off the target.

## Risks
- **US anti-dumping** annual reviews could raise duties for individual exporters and shift orders.
- **China pangasius demand** is sensitive to domestic tilapia oversupply and consumer income.
- **Ecuador shrimp competition** continues to compress raw-material premium for VN processors.

## Opportunities
- **VHC**'s value-added pangasius mix shift insulates it partially from raw-fillet price cyclicality.
- **MPC**'s vertical integration in shrimp is a margin defense if Ecuadoran prices firm.
- **Halal-certified line expansion** opens incremental Middle East / Indonesia demand.

## Timeline
- 2025-Q4: VASEP signals 12bn target.
- 2026-05-14: Public lobbying for trade-barrier removal intensifies.
- 2026-H2: Next US shrimp anti-dumping administrative review.
