# Bamboo Diplomacy

**One-liner:** Vietnam's named foreign-policy doctrine of strategic
balance — rooted yet flexible — maintaining productive relations across
geopolitically opposed great powers.

## What it is

Described in [[fdi-vietnam-fair-sustainable]] as the political enabler of
Vietnam's FDI attractiveness. Vietnam is one of the few countries holding
Comprehensive Strategic Partnerships with **all five UN Security Council
permanent members** (China, Russia, United States, France, United
Kingdom), plus 15 partner states in total — even where those states are
in tension with each other.

## How it works

Operationally, bamboo diplomacy translates into: (i) refusing alliance
blocks, (ii) signing parallel comprehensive strategic partnerships, and
(iii) negotiating FTAs across rival blocs (EVFTA with the EU, UKVFTA with
the UK, CPTPP and RCEP across the Asia-Pacific). The result, per [[fdi-
vietnam-fair-sustainable]], is that investors choose Vietnam for
*economic* reasons without political friction — a key resilience signal
in the friend-shoring era.

## Where it shows up

- In [[foreign-direct-investment]] as the political precondition that
  keeps both sides of the US–China divide invested
- In [[friend-shoring]] as the reason political-friendliness criteria do
  not block Vietnam

## Open debates

- Whether the doctrine remains viable as great-power competition
  sharpens.
- Whether FDI investors will continue accepting a "bamboo" posture as
  political risk rises.
