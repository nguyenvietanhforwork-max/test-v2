# Global Value Chain

**One-liner:** The cross-border sequence of value-adding activities — from
input to consumer — coordinated by lead firms; the structural unit
restructured under friend-shoring.

## What it is

In [[fdi-vietnam-fair-sustainable]], GVCs are framed as undergoing major
reorganisation under geopolitical and pandemic shocks. The policy
implication for Vietnam is twofold: (i) inserting domestic firms further
*up* the value chain (toward higher value-added activities) and (ii) lifting
domestic supplier quality, currently ranked 116/137 by the WEF.

## How it works

A GVC has a "smile curve" of value: design and R&D capture margin at one
end, assembly captures the lowest margin in the middle, and brand/sales
capture margin at the other end. Vietnam has historically participated at
the assembly node. The author's argument is that supplier-quality
upgrading, energy and logistics infrastructure, and administrative
simplification together unlock movement up the curve.

## Where it shows up

- In [[foreign-direct-investment]] as the structural target Vietnam is
  trying to penetrate further
- In [[friend-shoring]] as the unit being relocated
- In [[fedex-viettel-post-partnership]] as the logistics layer that
  operationalises GVC participation for cross-border e-commerce SMEs

## Open debates

- Whether GVC upgrading is feasible without indigenous R&D capacity.
- Whether services-FDI liberalisation is a prerequisite or a parallel
  track.
