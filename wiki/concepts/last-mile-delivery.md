# Last-Mile Delivery

**One-liner:** The final leg of physical distribution from a local
fulfilment node to the end customer — typically the costliest and
hardest-to-scale segment in cross-border logistics.

## What it is

The [[fedex-viettel-post-partnership]] is structured precisely around
last-mile execution: Viettel Post owns domestic pickup, delivery, and
warehousing as the **National Network Provider** for FedEx in Vietnam,
while FedEx retains the international leg.

## How it works

Last-mile economics depend on density (drops per stop), geographic
coverage, fleet utilisation, and address quality. Integrating with an
international carrier requires process alignment so that handover events
(customs, sort, dispatch) produce consistent shipment events visible in
both systems — a key challenge surfaced by Masamichi Ujiie in the source.

## Where it shows up

- In [[end-to-end-logistics]] as the closing node
- In [[cross-border-e-commerce]] as the success-or-failure point for SMEs

## Open debates
