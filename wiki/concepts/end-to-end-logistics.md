# End-to-End Logistics

**One-liner:** A logistics operating model that owns or coordinates every
node from origin pickup to destination delivery, including customs and
the data layer.

## What it is

In [[fedex-viettel-post-partnership]], end-to-end logistics is the
positioning language: FedEx and Viettel Post combine to "create a
seamless end-to-end supply chain" with optimised transit times and
extended pickup cut-offs.

## How it works

Operationally, E2E logistics is the product of three layers stacked:
physical network (fleet, sort centres, warehouses, last-mile vans), data
layer (real-time visibility, customs documents, shipment events), and
service layer (SLAs, exception handling). The challenge — explicitly
raised by Masamichi Ujiie — is that information must flow seamlessly
across two systems while service performance stays consistent across
geographies.

## Where it shows up

- In [[last-mile-delivery]] as the closing leg
- In [[cross-border-e-commerce]] as the integrated service customers buy
- In [[supply-chain-visibility]] as the visibility-layer requirement

## Open debates

- Whether single-provider E2E (vertical integration) outperforms
  partner-network E2E (the FedEx–Viettel Post pattern) at Vietnam scale.
