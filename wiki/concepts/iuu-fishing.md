# IUU Fishing

**One-liner:** Illegal, Unreported and Unregulated fishing — the
compliance regime (EU "yellow card", US import controls) governing
traceability for wild-catch seafood exports.

## What it is

In [[vasep-12-billion-seafood-2026]], IUU is the single biggest non-tariff
compliance pressure on Vietnam's wild-catch exports to the EU and US.
VASEP's policy submission centres on amending **Circulars 81/2025 and
74/2025** which currently impose friction on small-scale fisheries
attempting to comply with traceability requirements.

## How it works

IUU enforcement requires every exported lot to be traced to a legally
operating vessel with valid logbooks, vessel monitoring data, and
catch-area declarations. EU-bound product is gatekept against the
"yellow-card" regime; US-bound product faces import-control parallels.
Operational frictions raised in the source:

- "Whole-lot" definition for imported seafood mismatches commercial reality
- Fishing-zone partitioning forces >15m vessels with limited engine power
  to operate offshore, raising cost and shrinking legal raw-material supply
- Provincial implementation of the circulars is non-uniform, especially
  for small-scale fisheries

VASEP asks for digitalised workflows, simplified dossiers, harmonised
provincial implementation, and a revisited fishing-zone partitioning.

## Where it shows up

- In [[seafood-export]] as the central compliance gate
- In [[trade-defense]] as a non-tariff barrier overlapping with anti-
  dumping cases

## Open debates

- Whether stricter IUU rules raise or lower long-run market access.
- Whether small-scale fisheries can be compliant without compromising
  livelihoods.
